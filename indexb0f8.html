We proudly present our latest partner: Oxford University Computing Services

Wednesday, 20 February 2008

In January 2008 Oxford University Computing Services (OUCS) joined the Preparing DARIAH project. The OUCS manages and maintains the IT infrastructure that is necessary for a world class university. It also hosts the Oxford Text Archive, the British National Corpus and the OSSWatch Advisory Service, to name but a few.

DARIAH partners prepare for contract negotiations

Tuesday, 27 November 2007
On 26 November 2007 DARIAH partners met in Brussels to discuss activities for the next few months. During the meeting, EU project officer Saracco provided information on the current status of the Preparing DARIAH proposal.

Though nothing is official yet, it seems the invitation for the contract negotiations can be expected any time now. Important activities for the next few months will therefore be the preparation of a consortium agreement and improving the Preparing DARIAH proposal, taking into account the suggestions from the evaluators.

One of these suggestions concerns the specification of user communities. For the Preparing DARIAH project, the broad DARIAH target audience will be narrowed down to a number of specific user communities. The focus will be on the specific user communities of the two demonstrators that will initially be integrated in DARIAH:
• ARENA, with the specific user community of data creators from the field of archaeology;
• Manuscripta runica in the Arnamagnaean Collection, with the specific user community of data users from the field of history and text.

Another suggestion from the evaluators concerns the technical aspects of the infrastructure; more details are required. To this end, the technical working group presented a new version of the work package description in which technologies to be used are described in more detail.

The amount of co-financing to be expected from the EC is also important; the proposed maximum contribution is significantly less than the requested contribution. Partners will therefore look into possibilities to increase national funding.

Apart from the adjustments for the proposal, short term activities discussed include the preparation of a newsletter, upgrade of the website and the appointment of liaison officers that will form a link between DARIAH and relevant international (e-humanities) projects like CLARIN and ENRICH.

DARIAHs technical work packages have conducted a survey and raised awareness for DARIAH

Saturday, 15 September 2007
The technical work packages have conducted an initial survey among the DARIAH partners to find out more about the existing technical and conceptual expertise, and raised awareness for DARIAH at several conferences and workshops.

Survey shows differences in expertise among participating partners
This initial survey showed great differences in expertise, and it is expected to be one of the main challenges to at the same time support the work on national infrastructures in less advanced countries and to make existing and future national infrastructure work together.

The DARIAH technical work packages have already started to raise awareness for DARIAH
DARIAH was represented at several more technically oriented conferences and workshops such as the IEEE conference on e-Science and Grid technologies in Bangalore, and the Open Grid Forums 21 and 22, in Manchester and Seattle. Additionally, DARIAH was presented at workshops on cyber-infrastructures for arts and humanities at the Digital Humanities conference in Urbana-Champaign in 2007, and at the UK based conference on Digital Resource in the Arts and Humanities.

The feedback at these events has been overwhelmingly positive and supportive and showcased Europe as a research and development leader in the domain of research infrastructures for arts and humanities.
 

Max Planck Digital Library and Goettingen State and University Library (SUB) Goettingen sign a Cooperation Agreement for e-humanities

Tuesday, 15 May 2007
The Max Planck Digital Library (MPDL) and Goettingen State and University Library (SUB) have agreed to further the development of a digital infrastructure for the arts and humanities (eHumanities). The agreement was signed on May 3, 2007 at the German E-Science Conference 2007 in Baden-Baden.

Scholars in the arts and humanities require both a common infrastructure and domain-specific tools to conduct their scientific activities. The cooperation between the Max Planck Digital Library and the State and University Library Goettingen fundamentally supports the construction of such a novel infrastructure.

The digital infrastructure will include open access to primary digital resources, publications and supplementary material; the location-independent creation and edition of textual data; and long term preservation of digital information.
