Welcome

The mission of DARIAH is to enhance and support digitally-enabled research across the humanities and arts. DARIAH aims to develop and maintain an infrastructure in support of ICT-based research practices.

DARIAH is working with communities of practice to:

  • Explore and apply ICT-based methods and tools to enable new research questions to be asked and old questions to be posed in new ways
  • Improve research opportunities and outcomes through linking distributed digital source materials of many kinds
  • Exchange knowledge, expertise, methodologies and practices across domains and disciplines

We proudly present our latest partner: Oxford University Computing Services

Wednesday, 20 February 2008

In January 2008 Oxford University Computing Services (OUCS) joined the Preparing DARIAH project. The OUCS manages and maintains the IT infrastructure that is necessary for a world class university. It also hosts the Oxford Text Archive, the British National Corpus and the OSSWatch Advisory Service, to name but a few.

The Oxford University Computing Services’ extensive knowledge of digital project management and metadata standards will support the Preparing DARIAH project, and we are thrilled to have OUCS on board.

Preparing DARIAH invited to Grant Agreement negotiations

Monday, 17 December 2007
As an early Christmas-present, the European Commission has invited Preparing DARIAH to Grant Agreement negotiations; these negotiations should take place before the end of January 2008.

The first meeting will be held in Brussels with EU project officer Lorenza Saracco. DARIAH members are thrilled with the invitation and looking forward to the meeting.

Important activities for the next few weeks are preparing the Grant Agreement Preparation forms and the improved project description, taking into account the suggestions from the evaluators.
 

DARIAH partners prepare for contract negotiations

Tuesday, 27 November 2007
On 26 November 2007 DARIAH partners met in Brussels to discuss activities for the next few months. During the meeting, EU project officer Saracco provided information on the current status of the Preparing DARIAH proposal.

Though nothing is official yet, it seems the invitation for the contract negotiations can be expected any time now. Important activities for the next few months will therefore be the preparation of a consortium agreement and improving the Preparing DARIAH proposal, taking into account the suggestions from the evaluators.

One of these suggestions concerns the specification of user communities. For the Preparing DARIAH project, the broad DARIAH target audience will be narrowed down to a number of specific user communities. The focus will be on the specific user communities of the two demonstrators that will initially be integrated in DARIAH:
• ARENA, with the specific user community of data creators from the field of archaeology;
• Manuscripta runica in the Arnamagnaean Collection, with the specific user community of data users from the field of history and text.

Another suggestion from the evaluators concerns the technical aspects of the infrastructure; more details are required. To this end, the technical working group presented a new version of the work package description in which technologies to be used are described in more detail.

The amount of co-financing to be expected from the EC is also important; the proposed maximum contribution is significantly less than the requested contribution. Partners will therefore look into possibilities to increase national funding.

Apart from the adjustments for the proposal, short term activities discussed include the preparation of a newsletter, upgrade of the website and the appointment of liaison officers that will form a link between DARIAH and relevant international (e-humanities) projects like CLARIN and ENRICH.
