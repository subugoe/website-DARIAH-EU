OUCS - Oxford University Computing Services

 

Oxford University Computing Services manage and maintain the IT infrastructure needed to support research at a world class University. Key services at OUCS include:
• the Oxford Text Archive,
• the Text Encoding Initiative,
• the British National Corpus,
• the OSSWatch Advisory Service, and
• the Intute Arts and Humanities gateway.

OUCS has a long history of participation in digital applications in the Humanities, with particular expertise in:
• digital project management,
• text encoding,
• metadata standards,
• electronic editing and publishing technologies, and
• corpus linguistics.

Role in the Preparing DARIAH project
The Oxford University Computing Services participate in the dissemination work package. They will act as the liaison between DARIAH and CLARIN, ensuring a smooth flow of communications between the two projects, and will provide links with other key areas of linguistic, literary and textual scholarship.
