OpenEdition

 

OpenEdition offers the academic community four international-scale publication and information platforms in the humanities and social sciences:

  • Revues.org, a platform for the online publication of more than 300 electronic academic journals in the humanities and social sciences
  • OpenEdition Books, a platform for the publication of electronic book series, to be launched in 2012
  • Calenda, an open-access calendar announcing more than 18,000 events in the arts, humanities and social sciences.
  • Hypotheses.org, a platform hosting 300 research blogs, available via several linguistic platforms: de.hypotheses.org (German-language blogs) and es.hypotheses.org (Spanish-language blogs) for example.

OpenEdition also hosts LusOpenEdition (http://lusopenedition.org/), a portal for humanities and social sciences publications in Portuguese. This project is developed in partnership with the Centro em Rede de Investigação em Antropologia, and is co-funded by the Calouste Gulbenkian Foundation. LusOpenEdition seeks to implement an electronic publishing portal dedicated to the development and dissemination of scientific research results in the humanities in Portuguese-speaking countries.

The OpenEdition Freemium programme aims at financing Open Access to the academic literacy by commercializing exclusive services for libraries (access to PDF and Epub formats, services for subscribing libraries and their users). Journals and books which are part of the freemium programme publish in full Open Access the HTML format of their articles and chapters. Two-thirds of the income generated by libraries’ subscriptions is allocated to the journals and partner publishers who adopt the freemium model. Campuses who have subscribed to the OpenEdition Freemium package have exclusive access to PDF and Epub formats (for iPad, Kindle, etc.) and to services dedicated to their needs.

The OpenEdition portal is developed by the Centre for Open Electronic Publishing (Cléo) and has been granted the Equipex Future Investment Award by the French Ministry for Higher Education and Research (2012).

Please visit the website for further information http://www.openedition.org/
