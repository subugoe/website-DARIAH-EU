National Case Studies and Status Surveys

These studies are of interest to all those working towards the development of enhanced opportunities for collaborative and independent research in the arts and humanities. They present in-depth reports of conditions in various European states.

  • DARIAH Case Study of Ireland


    Date Added:
    09/21/2009

    This study aims to identify the development of a digital humanities infrastructure and to map all the relevant recommendations, strategies and initiatives put forward to enable the development of future policies in Ireland. The study identifies the key policy makers and planning processes and accounts for achievement and under-achievement to date. Further, it addresses the challenges to the future development of a digital humanities infrastructure in Ireland.

  • DARIAH Current Status Survey of Greece


    Date Added:
    03/01/2010

    This study assess the views of Greek humanities scholars with respect to the existence (or not) of a coherent national strategy in digital research in the humanities. The interviewees recognize the main obstacles for the implementation of a national digital infrastructure and provide an interesting self-assessment regarding Greece as compared to the level of other European countries. According to the conclusion of the report, the results of the questionnaire regarding the awareness of the organisations and their willingness to participate in broader initiatives are an encouraging sign of progress, but cannot alone guarantee a promising future for digital humanities in Greece.
