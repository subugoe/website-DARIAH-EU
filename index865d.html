TextGrid

 

The Virtual Research Environment TextGrid enables researchers in the text-based humanities - such as philologists working with digital editions - to create, analyse, edit, store and publish their research data in one (user) environment. It is designed for use in individual projects as well as for collaborative usage within a research network. TextGrid consists of two main components, the TextGrid Laboratory (TextGrid Lab) and the TextGrid Repository (TextGrid Rep).

The TextGrid Laboratory offers a variety of digital tools and services that are designed to cover complete scholarly workflows, from collecting / generating primary data to publishing and archiving. Amongst others, the Lab consists of an XML Editor, a text-image-link editor, a dictionary search tool, an image viewer and a workflow tool. TextGrid is extensible, allowing for the enhancement of existing tools and the plug-in of external tools and services.

The TextGrid Repository, a long-term archive for research data in the humanities, offers sustainable access to its research data as well as interoperability with other repositories and/or their content.

As an open source project, TextGrid is built on open standards and is well-documented through user and developer manuals both online and print, enabling the easy exchange and reusability of both data and tools.
