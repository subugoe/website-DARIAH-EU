DCU - Digital Curation Unit, Athena Research Centre

 

Athena R.C. was founded in Athens in 2001 by the Ministry of Development as a national research organisation for information, communication and knowledge technologies. It comprises five research institutes and two research units.

The Digital Curation Unit (DCU) is the interdisciplinary arm of the Athena Research Centre and is specialised in the lifecycle management of knowledge assets to ensure their future usability. It conducts research, develops technologies and applications, and provides services and training. The DCU combines expertise from the fields of:
• informatics,
• management and decision sciences,
• library science and archives management,
• material culture studies, museology,
• communications and
• law.

The Digital Curation Unit also acts as a national centre for digital curation in Greece in such fields as:
• cultural heritage,
• research repositories,
• e-government and
• organisational records management.

Role in the Preparing DARIAH project
The Digital Curation Unit contributes to the conceptual modelling work in DARIAH, especially analysis and design work on scholarly and scientific processes and discourse.
