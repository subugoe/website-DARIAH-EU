DANS - Data Archiving and Networked Services

 

Mission
To stimulate (re-)use of humanities and social sciences data in the Netherlands and to provide scholars in these fields with state-of-the-art knowledge and infrastructures for data preservation and dissemination.

Organization
DANS has been established as a Royal Netherlands Academy of Arts and Sciences (KNAW) institute, in cooperation with the Netherlands Organization for Scientific Research (NWO). At present DANS has about 20 permanent and 15 temporary staff members, working in the DANS office in The Hague.

Expertise and services
Available expertise covers both data preservation and data dissemination related aspects:
• metadata issues (standards),
• legal aspects (IPR, privacy),
• technological developments (persistent identifiers, repositories, grid-technologies) and
• dissemination activities (workshops, conferences, journals).

Services provided include:
• advice and guidance on setting up data collections;
• data-archiving facilities (EASY);
• development of long term preservation solutions (MIXED);
• coordination of data harmonization activities;
• stimulation of data re-use (Alfa-Geo).

Role in the Preparing DARIAH project
DANS is one of the initiators of DARIAH and coordinates the Preparing DARIAH project. A dedicated project manager has been appointed, but more DANS experts will contribute to the preparatory work.
